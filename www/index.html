Meat Thermometer 101

The numbers.

Trust a probe, not a guess. These are the internal temperatures and visual cues for each level of doneness. Pull the meat 5°F before target — it rises while resting.

Rare
120–125°F (49–52°C)

Cool, bright red center. Silky texture. Touch test: press your cheek — that's how it yields.

Medium-Rare
130–135°F (54–57°C)

Warm, pink-red center. Slightly firmer, juicy. The preferred choice for most steaks. Touch: fingertip to thumb.

Medium
140–145°F (60–63°C)

Pink throughout, brown outer band. Firm but still moist. Touch: middle finger to thumb.

Well Done
160°F+ (71°C+)

No pink. Fully browned throughout. Firmer texture. Touch: ring finger to thumb (ball of hand is tense).

USDA Minimum Safe Temps
Beef, Lamb (whole cuts) 145°F + 3 min rest
Pork 145°F + 3 min rest
Ground Meat 160°F
All Poultry 165°F
Fish / Seafood 145°F
Pro Tips
  • Insert probe into the thickest part, away from bone or fat
  • Pull 5°F early — carryover cooking raises temp while resting
  • Rest 5 min minimum (10 min for thick cuts). Cover loose with foil
  • Pat dry before grilling — steam is the enemy of a good sear
  • Room-temp meat (30 min out of fridge) cooks more evenly
  • Don't press the meat — you're squeezing out the juices
