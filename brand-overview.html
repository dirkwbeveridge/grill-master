Grill Master Brand Overview
The Story

Marcus bought a 1.5-inch ribeye from the butcher on Friday. He pulled it at 2pm. Patted it dry. Seasoned it with salt and left it on the counter.

The grill has been running for 20 minutes. It's high heat. He knows this part.

What he doesn't know: when to flip. How long on indirect. Whether the rest time he's been guessing at — four minutes, sometimes five — is actually doing anything. He's overcooked it twice this year. Both times he cut in too early, watched the pink disappear, and felt it.

He opens Grill Master.

Four taps: Beef. Ribeye. High. Med-Rare. He drags the slider to 1.5 inches. The results appear without ceremony: Side A 3:30. Flip 3:30. Indirect 2:00. Rest 7:30. Target temp 135°F.

He taps Start Cook. The screen goes to the timer. Large monospace numbers. A vertical ember line on the left. The stage label reads: Side A.

The grill is loud. He sets the phone on the side table, face up. He doesn't think about the time again.

3:30 later, the chime. The screen reads: Flip. He flips. One motion. Taps Confirm.

The cook runs. Indirect heat. The numbers count down. He's not watching. He trusts the sequence.

At Rest, the alarm fires. He pulls the steak. Sets it on the board. Waits. Seven minutes thirty seconds — the longest part, and for the first time he lets the full time run.

He cuts it at the two-thirds mark. Pink throughout to the edge. Exactly what he ordered at the butcher counter.

He names it. Saves it. Marcus Ribeye. One tap next time.

Brand Outline
What It Is

A science-backed grilling timer. Four-stage cook sequence — Sear Side A, Sear Side B, Indirect Heat, Rest — calculated from protein, cut, thickness, doneness level, and conditions. No guessing. No hardware.

Who It's For

Home cooks who take the grill seriously. They've been grilling for years. They own good equipment and buy quality meat. They still second-guess the timing. Grill Master is for the cook who wants to stop improvising and start repeating.

The Problem It Solves

Every cook has the same failure point: the flip decision and the pull decision. Guess wrong on either and a $28 ribeye becomes a lesson. Grill Master takes both decisions off the table. The timer tells you when. You execute.

The Promise
Cook it right. Every time.
Not faster. Not easier. Right. Grill Master is for people who care about the outcome, not the shortcut.
Voice — Pitmaster's Notebook
Imperatives
Commands, not invitations. Cut. Not Step 2: Select your cut. Flip. Not Time to Flip!
Numbers as facts
3:42 is not decorative. It's the answer. Every timer, temperature, and measurement in Courier monospace.
No enthusiasm
No exclamation points. No emoji. No "Let's grill!" energy. The app sounds like someone who has done this a thousand times.
Silence as voice
The app doesn't congratulate you. It tells you what to do next. Cook complete is two words, not a celebration.
Hierarchy by weight
Scale and weight carry meaning. The accent color appears once per screen. It orients. It doesn't decorate.

Motion

Mechanical, not bouncy. Transitions complete and stop. No spring physics. No delight animations. cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1) at 200ms. Stage transitions feel like a stage completing, not a level clearing.

What It Is Not

Not a recipe app. It does not suggest what to cook. It does not pair wine. It does not share your results. It is a precision instrument for one specific moment: the cook in progress. That's the entire product.

The story ends the same way every Saturday. Marcus pulls the steak, cuts it, and it's right. He puts his phone away. That's the whole product.
